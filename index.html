Frequently Asked Questions

Expert answers to common financial questions

Getting Started

Q: What's the difference between accounting and bookkeeping?
A: Bookkeeping is the daily recording of financial transactions. Accounting involves interpreting, analyzing, and summarizing that data to create financial statements and provide insights for decision-making.
Q: How do I start investing with a small amount?
A: Start with low-cost index funds or ETFs which provide instant diversification. Many brokers offer fractional shares. Focus on consistent contributions through dollar-cost averaging.

Financial Statements

Q: Why is cash flow more important than profit?
A: A company can be profitable on paper but still run out of cash. Profit includes non-cash items while cash flow shows actual money movement. You can't pay bills with "profit" - you need cash.
Q: How do I read a balance sheet?
A: Follow the equation: Assets = Liabilities + Equity. Look for positive working capital, reasonable debt levels, and growing equity over time.
