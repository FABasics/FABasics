Financial Accounting Terminology

Financial Statements
Balance Sheet
A statement of the assets, liabilities, and capital of a business or other organization at a particular point in time, detailing the balance of income and expenditure over the preceding period.
Example: The balance sheet on December 31st showed $50,000 in cash assets.
Income Statement (Profit and Loss)
A financial statement that shows a company's financial performance over a specific period. It summarizes revenues, costs, and expenses incurred during the period.
Example: The income statement revealed a net profit of $10,000 for the quarter.
Statement of Cash Flows
A financial statement that provides aggregate data regarding all cash inflows a company receives from its ongoing operations and external investment sources.
Example: The cash flow statement showed a net increase in cash from operating activities.
Core Concepts
Asset
Resources owned or controlled by a company that are expected to provide future economic benefits.
Example: Cash, Accounts Receivable, Equipment, Buildings.
Liability
An obligation that a company owes to outside parties, such as creditors, suppliers, or employees.
Example: Accounts Payable, Loans Payable, Unearned Revenue.
Equity
The residual interest in the assets of an entity that remains after deducting its liabilities. Often called owners' equity or stockholders' equity.
Example: Common Stock, Retained Earnings.
Revenue
The income generated from normal business activities, such as sales of goods or services.
Example: Sales revenue from products sold during the month.
Expense
The cost of operations that a company incurs to generate revenue.
Example: Rent expense, Salary expense, Utility expense.

Journal Entry
The means by which transactions are recorded in the accounting system, using debits and credits.
Example: A journal entry was made to debit cash and credit service revenue for a payment received.
Depreciation
The accounting method used to allocate the cost of a tangible asset over its useful life.
Example: The office equipment is depreciated over five years using the straight-line method.
GAAP
Generally Accepted Accounting Principles; the standard set of guidelines for financial accounting used in the U.S.
Example: The company must follow GAAP rules when preparing its annual financial statements.
