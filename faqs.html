Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common questions about accounting and finance concepts.

Q: What is the main difference between an Asset and a Liability?

A:

An **Asset** is something you *own* that puts money in your pocket (like cash or a machine). A **Liability** is something you *owe* that takes money out of your pocket (like a loan or a bill).

Q: Why do companies use Accrual Accounting?

A:

Accrual accounting gives a better picture of a company's performance by matching **revenue** (money earned) with the **expenses** (costs) that helped earn it, even if the cash hasn't moved yet. It's more honest than just tracking cash.

Q: What is P/E Ratio used for on the stock market?

A:

The Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio is like a price tag on a stock. It tells you how many dollars investors are willing to pay for every one dollar of a company's profit. A high P/E often means investors expect high future growth!
